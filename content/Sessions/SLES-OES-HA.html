SLES+OES+HA server cluster to support charities

Prof. DOUPNIK, Joe - Ingotec, MindworksUK, Univ of Oxford

Presentation - Bonaire 7, Friday, 4:00 p.m. to 4:45 p.m.

Providing quality file serving, email, groupware etc. services to charities encounters the problem of "only at very low cost, including upkeep and backups".

My small company, Ingotec, is proceeding along two pathways. One is OpenSUSE with email, eGroupWare suite, etc using only local drives and backed up by manually swapping between a pair of servers. The second pathway adds GroupWise with its database, for which we have had to go to SLES 11 SP2 with its HA facilities to make a two node cluster with only local drives, again with all the POSIX apps. HA was a challenge and worth the benefits. The problem is what can be done at very low cost indeed yet provide modern quality dependable service.

This talk describes the design processes, the what, why and how, and what we can achieve with open source.
